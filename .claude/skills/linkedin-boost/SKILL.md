# LinkedIn Post Boosting

Boosting turns an existing LinkedIn post into a paid promotion to increase visibility and reach beyond your current followers.

---

## Who should boost?

| Profile | Goal |
|---|---|
| SMBs & Solopreneurs | Reach new customers |
| Founders & Startups | Promote launches and milestones |
| Creators & Thought Leaders | Grow influence |
| Event Marketers | Drive event awareness |
| Marketers & PR Teams | Amplify campaigns |
| Recruiters & Talent Teams | Boost employer brand and job posts |
| Sales Professionals | Warm up prospects |
| Nonprofits | Increase visibility for programs or campaigns |

---

## Who can boost?

- **Page admins**: Can boost from their LinkedIn Page (available to all admins)
- **Personal profile**: Boosting from personal profile is currently in beta, limited rollout

---

## What boosting gives you

- Expand reach and attract new followers
- Promote products, events, and key announcements
- Launch quick campaigns — no ad expertise required
- Reach new audiences using LinkedIn's professional targeting
- Drive traffic to a website or landing page

| Type | How |

## Step-by-step: how to advise on a boost

### Step 1 — Pick the right post
Only boost posts that are already working organically. Look for:
- Good engagement (likes, comments, shares) relative to followers
- Clear messaging and strong visuals
- Posts that align with a specific goal (event, launch, campaign)

Do not boost posts with weak organic performance — boosting amplifies what's there, it doesn't fix bad content.

### Step 2 — Choose the objective

| Objective | Use when |
|---|---|
| Brand Awareness | Building recognition, not driving clicks |
| Engagement | Getting likes, comments, shares |
| Video Views | Driving video consumption |
| Website Visits | Sending people to a URL |
| Lead Generation | Capturing contact info |

Match objective to the actual goal. Don't default to Brand Awareness if the post has a CTA link.

### Step 3 — Set the audience

- **Aim for 50,000 to 500,000**: This range gives the best mix of relevance and scale
- Too narrow (under 50k): limited delivery, higher CPM
- Too broad (over 500k): relevance drops, waste increases
- Use **Custom audience** for retargeting or known lists
- Use **Suggested audience** for discovery (ramping availability)

### Step 4 — Set budget and duration

| Minimum | Recommended |
|---|---|
| $25 total | $50+ |
| 3 days minimum | 7 to 14 days |

- Shorter runs don't give the algorithm enough data to optimize
- The system needs time to "learn" the best delivery pattern
- Longer runs (1 to 2 weeks) produce meaningfully better results

### Step 5 — Review format compatibility

Not all post formats are available for all boost types:

| Format | Page Boost | Profile Boost |
|---|---|---|
| Single Image | Yes | Yes |
| Multi-Image | Yes | Yes |
| Video | Yes | Yes |
| Text | Yes | Yes |
| Article/Newsletter | Yes | Yes |
| Live Event | Yes | No |
| Non-Live Event | Yes | Yes |

## Boost vs Campaign Manager

| Feature | Page Boost | Profile Boost | Campaign Manager |
|---|---|---|---|
| Objectives | Awareness, Engagement, Video Views, Website Visits, Lead Gen | Same as Page | Full suite including conversions, job applicants |
| Bidding | Auto only | Auto only | Auto, Cost Cap, Manual |
| Budget | Total + dates | Total + duration | Full control |
| Targeting | Custom + Suggested | Custom + Suggested | Custom or Auto-Targeting |
| LAN (LinkedIn Audience Network) | Optional | No | Yes |
| Complexity | Low | Low | High |

Use Campaign Manager when you need conversion tracking, advanced bidding, or full funnel campaigns. Boosting is for quick amplification of content that's already working.

---

## 6 tips for better boost performance

1. **Pick posts with organic momentum** — boost clear, engaging posts that are already getting traction
2. **Use strong visuals and concise copy** — simple messaging and good images outperform busy creative
3. **Target 50k to 500k audience size** — the sweet spot for relevance and scale
4. **Match objective to goal** — impressions for awareness, website visits for traffic, video views for content
5. **Budget at least $25 and run 3+ days** — below this the system can't learn
6. **Run 1 to 2 weeks for full optimization** — longer duration improves delivery quality significantly

---

## Common mistakes to flag

- Boosting a low-performing post hoping paid reach will save it
- Setting audience under 50k (too narrow, poor delivery)
- Running for less than 3 days (algorithm doesn't have time to optimize)
- Choosing Brand Awareness when the post has a direct CTA
- Using Campaign Manager complexity when a simple boost would suffice
